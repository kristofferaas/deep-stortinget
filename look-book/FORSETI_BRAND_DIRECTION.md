# FORSETI

## Name

**Primary product name:** `Forseti`

**Why this name works:**

- Short, strong, and memorable
- Rooted in Norse tradition (justice, mediation, order)
- Feels credible for policy and democratic infrastructure
- Works in both Norwegian and international contexts

## Core Philosophy

`Forseti` exists to make public power legible.

Government should not be a black box. Parliamentary debates, hearings, votes, legal drafts, and spoken records belong to everyone. Forseti turns institutional complexity into public clarity without dumbing anything down.

## Mission

Make Norwegian democratic data understandable, searchable, and actionable for everyone.

## Product Promise

- **Open by default:** every insight links back to source material
- **AI with accountability:** summaries are explainable and traceable
- **Built for all levels:** from experts to first-time civic readers
- **Democracy as infrastructure:** clarity is a public good, not a premium add-on

## Emotional Feeling

The product should feel:

- Calm
- Serious
- Empowering
- Modern
- Trustworthy
- Civic, not corporate

Avoid feeling:

- Hypey
- Chaotic
- Partisan
- Overdesigned
- Cold bureaucracy

## Brand Personality

`Forseti` is:

- The clear translator of hard public information
- Neutral, evidence-first, and transparent
- Intellectually rigorous but easy to approach

Voice qualities:

- Clear over clever
- Precise over vague
- Confident over loud
- Human over academic jargon

## Design Direction

### Visual DNA

- Editorial clarity + modern product precision
- Strong typographic hierarchy
- Structured layouts with intentional whitespace
- Data-rich surfaces with calm visual rhythm
- Distinct Nordic tone: clean, cool, grounded

### Color feeling

Use a palette that suggests institutions, nature, and trust.

Example direction:

- `Fjord Ink` (deep blue-green) for trust and structure
- `Stone` neutrals for readability and seriousness
- `Signal Mint` or `Amber` as functional accents for insights and status
- Avoid flashy gradient-heavy startup cliches

### Typography direction

- Display: high-character serif or sharp grotesk for authority
- Body: highly readable sans for dense policy text
- Prioritize readability at long lengths (policy, legal context, transcripts)

### Motion direction

- Purposeful and restrained
- Use motion to clarify state changes, not for decoration
- Suggested pattern: staggered reveal + subtle transitions

## UX Principles

- **Source first:** never separate claims from evidence
- **Context always:** every summary must answer "what changed, why it matters"
- **Progressive depth:** quick overview first, deep detail one click away
- **Neutral framing:** show multiple positions fairly
- **Public accessibility:** plain language mode should always be available

## Information Model Anchors

Primary content objects Forseti should revolve around:

- Cases
- Hearings
- Votes
- Debate transcripts
- Bill revisions
- Committee notes
- Public consultations
